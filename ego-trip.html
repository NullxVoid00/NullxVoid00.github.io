Hub Character

MENDER

The mysterious bartender of Mender's Haven, serving as guide and father figure to lost souls. Charismatic yet enigmatic, Mender offers refuge, quests, and gentle pushing toward confronting deeper truths. His very name suggests healing, though his methods sometimes involve forcing difficult realizations.

As fragments venture deeper into the labyrinth's distractions, Mender grows concerned they may lose themselves entirely rather than face what lies beneath. Under certain conditions, this caring bartender becomes an optional boss fight - testing whether his charges are ready for the truth.

The Truth of N_

An echo of the discarded fragment Null wished to reject, N_ represents the trauma and responsibilities the host couldn't bear. The labyrinth was built as their prison, designed to keep them buried at the very bottom where their memories could never surface.

Unlike the other echoes, N_ retained a fully formed personality and clear memories. Their sadness and rage at being abandoned gave birth to The Shadow - a hulking mass of darkness that protects them. After countless failed escape attempts, N_ now waits for Null's arrival, seeking either escape or revenge.

The final boss battle involves both N_ and The Shadow, with players needing to attack the red shard in The Shadow's chest while N_ fights alongside it using various remnant abilities. The outcome determines which of seven possible endings the party achieves.

LOST SOULS & BOSSES

Powerful entities who entered the labyrinth and lost themselves to its corruption, each building their own twisted domain from their pain and obsessions.

THE SERPENT

The Fallen Artist

A giant snake coiled in crimson skies, its back serving as a treacherous pathway to its head. Once a young artist whose Ouroboros series brought fame, until false plagiarism accusations destroyed their reputation and broke their heart - literally.

Fight Mechanics: Players traverse the serpent's spine while destroying weak points and avoiding flying enemies. As health drops, spikes and rotating obstacles make the climb increasingly dangerous. The red, swirling sky pulses with intense music as the battle reaches its climax.

"I just wanted to create something beautiful... Why did they have to take that away from me?"

THE QUEEN

The Reluctant Ruler

A headless Victorian woman with multiple rotating arms serving as platforms, her decapitated head flying around attacking players. Born to rule but yearning for freedom, her attempts at benevolent leadership led only to her citizens' revolt and execution by guillotine.

Fight Mechanics: Scale the rotating hands safely to reach the flying head's weak points. As health drops, hands move faster and become trapped. The battle requires precise timing and platforming skills while avoiding the head's aerial assaults.

"I never wanted the crown... I just wanted to see the flowers bloom in peace."

THE CHARADE

The Emotional Executive

A massive business-suited entity with three giant floating masks covering its face. Once an ambitious businessman whose emotional instability cost him job after job, until expressing anger at the wrong person in a sleazy casino led to his death.

Fight Mechanics: Attack patterns change based on which mask is worn. Anger turns floors to lava, Sadness creates bullet-platform patterns, Happiness fires beam attacks. Must be destroyed in order: Sadness ‚Üí Anger ‚Üí Happiness, or the masks reform.

"Why is feeling things so wrong? I just wanted to make Ma and Pa proud..."

THE MOON

The Lost Child

A giant moon hanging above an endless ocean, shifting phases while controlling watery hands and obstacles below. Once a young girl who, after losing her mother and facing abuse, tried to swim to the moon's reflection to reunite with her mother's soul.

Fight Mechanics: The Moon attacks with different patterns based on its phases while controlling the ocean below. Eventually lowers itself and grasps its own body with giant water hands - the only opportunity to attack while avoiding its defenses.

"Mama said souls go to the Moon when they die... Why couldn't I reach you, Mama?"

MENDER (Optional/Secret)

The Concerned Caretaker

The beloved bartender himself becomes a formidable opponent when certain conditions are met. Quick and agile with high health, he uses area attacks, bullet patterns, and can separate players for one-on-one confrontations.

Fight Mechanics: Straightforward direct combat, but with massive health and immunity to most status effects. Can incapacitate separated players. This battle tests if the fragments are truly ready to face deeper truths or if they're still running from reality.

"Sometimes the kindest thing I can do is show you that you're stronger than you think."

The Corruption Effect

The labyrinth's mysterious influence twists powerful beings who venture too deep, making them act irrationally and aggressively. This corruption emanates from the very bottom of the maze, where a rejected fragment's growing instability affects all who enter.

After defeat, many bosses appear in Mender's Bar as miniaturized versions with their minds mostly restored, having been freed from the corruption that consumed them. There, they can finally find the peace that eluded them in their twisted domains.

The Red Shard's True Purpose

A Prison of Trauma

The Red Shard exists as a crystalline space between consciousness and dream, but its true purpose is far darker. It serves as a prison designed to contain N_ - the echo of Null's rejected fragment, Neuro. The labyrinth within was built specifically to keep N_ buried at the very bottom, ensuring their traumatic memories could never surface.

Unlike the other echo-fragments who are mere impressions, N_ retained full personality and clear memories of what Null tried to bury. Their growing instability and rage at abandonment created The Shadow - a hulking mass of darkness that protects them and serves as the source of the labyrinth's corrupting influence.

The Seven Endings

HUSK ENDING (Null Solo)

Null defeats The Self alone but, after glimpsing traumatic imagery in the Red Shard, loses all interest in it. They return to Mender's to repeat the labyrinth indefinitely, becoming an empty shell going through motions.

OBSESSION ENDING (Null + _L)

Null and _L defeat The Self together, but Limerence convinces Null to stay and repeat the labyrinth in pursuit of meaning they'll never find. The search becomes more important than any destination.

NARCISSIST ENDING (Null + _M)

Null and _M confront the Red Shard, but Midas shows Null all the treasures and remnants they've collected, convincing them to stay and gather riches until the end of time. Material accumulation becomes the only goal.

COWARD ENDING (Null + _D)

Null and _D defeat The Self, but Dolor freezes in absolute terror at what lies ahead and begs Null to stay. They return to Mender's, never to leave again, paralyzed by fear of growth.

MISSING ENDING (Null + All 3 Fragments)

With all fragments present, they attempt to break through the Red Shard together but fail. The group is left eternally trying to breakthrough, having the pieces but lacking the knowledge to proceed.

DROWN ENDING (All Fragments, No Notes)

Without sufficient understanding from collected notes, the echo fragments turn on Null upon reaching the shard, grabbing them and forcing their body into the crystal. Null drowns in the red liquid, leading to a restart.

TRUE ENDING (All Fragments + All Notes)

With complete knowledge, sense of self, and resilience built over the journey, the echoes celebrate and reintegrate with Null. Together they shatter the Red Shard and dive into the red pool created, ready to begin their true journey in Ego/Dive.

The Connection to Ego/Dive

A Necessary Prelude

Ego/Trip serves as both power fantasy and preliminary journey - a consequence-free space for processing trauma in abstract, game-like manner. The Red Shard acts as a waiting room between consciousness and deeper unconsciousness, where Null can build strength before facing the weightier emotional confrontations awaiting in Absentia.

Only by achieving the True Ending - gathering complete understanding through notes and working with all aspects of themselves - can Null break free from this crystalline refuge and dive into the layered world of Ego/Dive, where their real journey of healing or destruction begins.

The echo-fragments serve as practice versions of the full personalities Null will encounter in Absentia. Mender's gentle guidance prepares them for the harsher tests ahead, while the corrupted bosses offer glimpses of what happens when trauma consumes completely.

The Labyrinth's Origin

A Prison Built from Denial

The endless, hostile labyrinth was constructed by Null's own psyche as a way to ensure N_ could never escape and bring their traumatic memories to the surface. Every creature, trap, and shifting room serves as another barrier designed to send N_ back to their starting point at the maze's bottom.

The corruption affecting powerful beings who venture too deep emanates directly from N_'s Shadow and their building instability. As N_ grew more desperate and angry over countless failed escape attempts, their influence began twisting other lost souls into the bosses players encounter.

When Null and the echo-fragments finally arrive, N_ senses their presence and waits - no longer trying to escape, but preparing for confrontation. Whether this leads to revenge, understanding, or integration depends entirely on the choices made throughout the journey.
